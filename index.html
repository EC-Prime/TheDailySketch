Grid layouts reshape how developers build the modern web

For years, laying out a page meant wrestling floats or stacking flexbox containers three levels deep just to get a sidebar in the right place. CSS Grid changed the conversation entirely, letting developers name entire regions of a page and drop content into them directly. What once took nested divs and careful ordering now takes a single grid-template-areas declaration.

Junior developers are shipping portfolio projects faster than ever

Bootcamp graduates and self-taught coders are increasingly skipping tutorial-only learning in favour of small, complete projects that mirror real production work. Recruiters say a working full-stack app, however small, tells them more in five minutes than a resume tells them in five pages.

React remains the most requested frontend skill

Job postings across South Africa continue to list React ahead of Vue and Angular combined, according to recent hiring trend reports.

PostgreSQL adoption climbs among startups

Development teams are increasingly favouring PostgreSQL over MySQL for new projects, citing stronger support for complex queries and JSON data.

APIs power the next generation of side projects

From movie trackers to weather dashboards, public APIs are letting solo developers build data-rich apps without needing to design a backend from scratch.
